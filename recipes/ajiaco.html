Ajiaco

Description

>The autochthonous Ajiaco is a Santafereña soup based on chicken, capira, pastusa and criolla potatoes,

cobs and guasca, accompanied by milk cream and capers, rice and avocado. Ajiaco is one of those dishes that you cannot miss on your visit to Colombia.

Ingredients

  • 1 small chicken breast without skin but with the bone (400 g)
  • ¼ pound Creole potato, peeled and sliced (125 g) ¼ pound pastusa potato, peeled and sliced (125 g)
  • 3 ears of corn, halved (380 g)
  • 1/2 bunch of guascas (13 g)
  • 4 tablespoons of MILK CREAM

Steps

  1. bring it to medium heat and stir constantly, add to the pot: the cob, the bone-in chicken breast and the potatoes. Cook over high heat for 20 minutes or until the potato and breast are cooked.
  2. Remove the chicken breast from the pot and let it cool a bit to shred it. Once you have shredded the chicken, add the guascas to the pot and let them cook for 5 more minutes or until the potato melts a little and thus the Ajiaco thickens more.
  3. Serve the Santafereño Ajiaco in 4 equal plates, add the shredded chicken on top, decorate with a spoonful of MILK CREAM

Accompany it with avocado AND ENJOY
